Apple Crumble

This apple crumble is a fall staple - warm and gooey on the inside with a crunchy, cinnamon-y top. Make sure to have some ice cream (vanilla bean is the best) to serve with it!

I highly recommend using Granny Smith apples for this recipe (and for baking in general). Granny Smiths are great for baking because they don't dissolve into mush when baked. Apple crumble should have apple chunks, not applesauce!

Ingredients

Crumble Topping

  • 1 cup flour
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar (light or dark)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter, melted

Filling

  • 2 1/2 lbs Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp corn starch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375 °F. Lightly spray an 8 or 9-inch baking dish with nonstick cooking spray.

Make Crumble Topping

  1. In a medium bowl, combine flour, brown sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
  2. Add melted butter and stir with a fork until crumbly.

Prepare Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine apples, granulated sugar, lemon juice, cornstarch, and cinnamon.
  2. Transfer apple mixture into prepared baking dish and spread into an even layer.
  3. Sprinkle crumble topping evenly over the apple mixture.
  4. Bake for 35-45 minutes or until bubbling and golden brown on top.
  5. Let cool for 15 minutes. Serve with ice cream on top!
