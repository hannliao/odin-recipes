Banana Bread

Description

Your bananas are turning brown! Don't throw them away - turn them into a scrumptious banana bread. This chocolatey banana loaf is perfect for any occasion: eat a slice for breakfast or bring the whole loaf to a party to share with friends.

For this banana bread, I recommend using overripe bananas, since they tend to be sweeter. Feel free to use any kind of chocolate chips, but I like using mini semi-sweet chocolate chips (they spread out the chocolate taste more evenly!).

Ingredients

  • 5 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 overripe bananas
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • pinch of salt
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 °F. Coat a loaf pan with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. Peel bananas and place them on a plate. Crush them with a fork until they are thoroughly mashed.
  3. In a medium bowl, cream together butter and sugar.
  4. Add eggs, vanilla, and smashed bananas. Mix until just combined.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  6. Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients. Mix until just combined.
  7. Add chocolate chips. Mix until just combined.
  8. Pour batter into prepared loaf pan.
  9. Bake for 40-45 minutes. Check that consistency is not liquidy with a toothpick inserted in the middle of the loaf.
